Foreword:

This Notebook serves to deliver a few insights into the World of our Brainwaves. For this purpose, I recorded two Subjects (anonym). Both Subjects have been asked to do the same task under the same conditions, without knowing from each other.

Setting of the experiment:

  • a calm room, without any option of destruction outside the room
  • reading the same book, starting from the Introduction, which they didn't know before. The book is called: "The Vital Question" by Nick Lane

Idea of the experiment:

  • figuring out if it is possible to see obvious differences between both subjects
  • are the results usable to develop on the long term algorithms to help people with focusing and meditation
  • exploring the world of our brainwaves to capture a better grasp about the topic
  • state a Hypothesis if it is possible to make statements about their level of focusing on the book
  • explore if someone outside the experiment can tell with a couple of information, which person was more focused during the task.

Abstract:

Objective:

To record the brainwaves I used the Muse Headband and the App called Muse-Monitor. The App allows to access the raw EEG Output (data) of the device and to save it as a CSV file. EEG (Electroencephalography) measures voltage fluctuations resulting from ionic current within the neurons of the brain. Clinically, EEG is most commonly referred to diagnose sleep disorders, epilepsy, coma, brain death and more. Today, thanks to companies like Muse and Emotiv and their Developer Kits, the benefits of EEG are now publicly available. For example, to help people improving their focus, meditation, sleep and even to control and play video games or VR sceneries.

Hint: Relevant for identifying if a person is concentrated and focused: In this Notebook only β-Waves & γ-Waves are observed.

Methods:

I decided to base my methods on the EDA as mentioned above. EDA is a commonly performed method to first, understand the data and to gather as many insights as possible from it. Once the dataset is cleaned there are many different approaches to visualize the data. I decided on using matplotlib and seaborn, since they are great libraries to visualize (color mapping) and explore the data fast and efficiently. Many different plots were used to display and compare both Datasets to eventually conclude.

Hint: The data cleaning approach is in this Notebook only referenced by the Dataset from Subject 1. The Dataset from the second proband (Subject 2) was already prepared beforehand with the same methods to make this Notebook more readable.

Further the Subject 2 Dataset will be loaded later for comparing the data.

Results:

The EDA outcome displays very clear the differences of the brainwave types, as well as the differences within the brain activity of both subjects. Both were during their task, focused and concentrated.

Conclusion:

Hint: For not being offensive, both probands were focused on their individual level.

Only by separately visualizing both Datasets as displayed in the graphs of Fig.3 and Fig.5 it is difficult to tell, whom of the subjects was more focused. Nevertheless, by generating dashed density plots and compare them for both subjects showed in Fig.4 and Fig.4.1 of β-Waves as well as Fig.5 and Fig.5.1 of γ-Waves, that it is much clearer to see that both were processing information and in a state of learning. In fact, figures of Part 11 and 11.1 as well as the sns.kdeplot in Fig. 8 supports the general idea to state if it is possible to see with EEG which person is, in general, more focused during their task(s).

Reviewing the evidence, to come up with remarkable results to develop algorithms in order to help people being more focused and understand their own brainwaves, as well as revealing whom of both individuals in this experiment is in general more concentrated, there are much more trials needed. These includes also all available measurement Channels from the specific device. Besides, the same conditions need to bet set within the scope of measurement accuracy to eventually determine which person is better in focusing.

Introduction:

The general idea of this experiment is to visualize both Datasets of Brainwaves from two probands to figure out if it is possible with the help of Data Science to say if one of the test-persons is less focused and concentrated than the other.

Keep in mind, the purpose behind this experiment is not to show, whom of the subjects is cognitive more capable of resolving tasks or staying longer in the state of deep learning, but if it is actually feasible to develop special algorithms, similar to the approach of the Company Muse within a student project. Independently of the provided recording device to help kids and adults train their brain, to finally stay longer concentrated or to improve the learning curve during meditation. To target this goal from the perspective of a data scientist many methods were applied during the EDA. Such as cleaning the data, plotting with matplotlib and seaborn.
The critical role is to overlapp the waves with each other and of both subjects to directly compare the visualized results in order to claim the Hypothesis mentioned before.
